Open Source

Approachable tech talk for creatives

Timeframe: 10 weeks

    Skills:
  • Layout
  • Illustration
  • Production
    Tools:
  • InDesign
  • Photoshop
  • Illustrator

The OPPORTUNITY

Many technology and science magazines currently exist, from Wired to Popular Science, but there has yet to be a technology magazine for creatives. Experience, interaction and web technologies are in constant flux and require professionals in those fields to stay up to date with current trends, technologies and systems.

Project goals

  • Create a technology magazine that is accessible, interesting and useful
  • Offer beginning, intermediate, and advanced content and tutorials
  • Create a reader base that is informed about the changing technology landscape, without buzzwords

The Process

Personas and research:

I conducted a series of interviews with technology workers, both designers and developers. After obtaining my qualitative research, I created three personas that would inform the content and tone of Open Source Magazine.

Content Sourcing:

I collected articles I wanted to feature, as well as images and advertisements that fit the magazines message. After I collected my assets I began to illustrate the geometric components that would visually unite the spreads.

Layout:

Now that I had all my components I began to deconstruct my type choices. I made sure the body copy was easily readable and properly leaded, and feed all my articles into the document. The rest of the process involved exploring expressive headlines, designing a layout system for images, pull quotes, and captions and fixing all microtype issues.

The Solution

Open Source Magazine informs creatives about new and upcoming technology through a creative lense. Bridging the gap between creativity and technology can seem orinious but Open Source Magazine is accessible, understandable, and closes that gap with ease. Open Source Magazine will keep every reader up to date in the ever-changing world of interaction and front-end technologies.
