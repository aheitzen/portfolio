The Opportunity

The Seattle Street Food Festival is an annual food truck gathering in SLU. Its core brand and advertising do not reach people outside of SLU which hurts festival turnout and small businesses who sell at the event. Additionally, local shopping and The Emerald City Night Market, a large party at the end of the night with local djs, are not advertised well and are often glossed over by festival attendees.

Project goals

  • Complete an advertising identity that informs and attracts attendees to the festival
  • Produce an updated logo, set of three headlines, magazine ads and digital ads
  • Produce billboards, merchandise, and a gorilla marketing item

The Process

Personas and research:

The first step involved researching the Seattle Street Food Festival. I completed a deep dive into who sponsored the event, what food trucks attended, and the alcohol policy. After asking these questions I was able to move confidently into visual design research. I knew I wanted a tone that felt hip, young, and tasty.

Iteration

Next I created a mini design system that I would pull components from to create all advertising assets for different channels. I chose a script font for the logo and a condescended bold font for copy, to give the festival a trendy tone. My colors are bright, saturated, and appetizing, perfect for a summer food festival in Seattle.

Completion:

Using my visual building blocks, I created an advertising system that can be applied to multiple channels and media.

The Solution

By creating an advertising campaign for the Seattle Street Food Festival, I hope to increase festival visibility and attendee turnout. The ad campaign is irreverent, fun, and features all aspects of the festival, not just the food trucks. By creating a holistic design system I was able to apply the same tone and messaging to multiple advertising channels.
